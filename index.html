Tutorial Companion

Simulating the 4.0V Epsom-Salt Lead-Acid Battery

Step 1: Placement In Progress

Add 1x USB Power Supply and 2x DIY Epsom-Salt Cells to the workspace.

Step 2: Series Connection Locked

Wire the cells in series: Connect the Positive (+) terminal of Cell 1 to the Negative (-) terminal of Cell 2.

Step 3: Current Protection Locked

Add a Resistor and set its value close to 81Ω to limit the initial forming current.

Step 4: Forming Charge Locked

Connect the USB power through the resistor to charge and form the series cells up to 4.0V total.

Step 5: Load Testing Locked

Disconnect the charger. Add a 330Ω Resistor and a Green LED to test the battery output discharge cycle.

Electrochemical State

Active Ions:Mg2+ , SO42-
Anode Chemistry:PbO₂ (Formed) / Pb
Cathode Chemistry:Spongy Lead (Pb)
Safe Operating Max:~2.05 V per cell
